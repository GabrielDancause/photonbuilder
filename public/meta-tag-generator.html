Frequently Asked Questions

What are HTML meta tags and why are they important?

Meta tags are snippets of text that describe a page's content. They don't appear on the page itself, but only in the page's HTML source code. They are essential for SEO because they tell search engines what a web page is about, influencing how your site appears in search results and whether users decide to click on it.

What is the ideal length for a Title Tag and Meta Description?

For optimal display in search engine results, it's recommended to keep your Title Tag between 50-60 characters and your Meta Description between 150-160 characters. Exceeding these limits may result in search engines truncating (cutting off) your text with an ellipsis (...).

What are Open Graph tags?

Open Graph (OG) tags are meta tags specifically designed to control how URLs are displayed when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, and Discord. By using OG tags, you can define the exact title, description, and image that appear in the social card, making your shared links more engaging.

Do I still need to use the meta keywords tag?

No, major search engines like Google abandoned the meta keywords tag for ranking purposes many years ago due to keyword stuffing abuse. While our tool allows you to add them for legacy systems or specific internal search engines, they will not improve your modern Google SEO rankings.

What does the viewport meta tag do?

The viewport meta tag instructs the browser on how to control the page's dimensions and scaling on different devices. Including <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0"> is critical for mobile responsiveness, ensuring your website adapts correctly to smartphones and tablets.
