The Importance of Typography

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. The arrangement of type involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing, and adjusting the space between pairs of letters.

Good typography establishes a strong visual hierarchy, provides a graphic balance to the website, and sets the product's overall tone. It should guide and inform your users, optimize readability and accessibility, and ensure an excellent user experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the best font pairing for my website?

The best font pairings usually contrast but complement each other. A common approach is pairing a serif font for headings with a sans-serif font for body text, or vice versa. Ensure the fonts share a similar x-height or geometric structure for visual harmony.

What is readability scoring in typography?

Readability scoring evaluates how easy it is to read text based on font choice, line height, letter spacing, and contrast. Good readability ensures users can consume your content without eye strain.

Why is loading performance important for web fonts?

Web fonts can slow down your site if they are too large or if you load too many font weights. Optimizing font loading improves your Core Web Vitals, reduces layout shifts (CLS), and provides a faster experience for users.

What are web safe fonts vs Google Fonts?

Web safe fonts are pre-installed on most operating systems (like Arial or Times New Roman). Google Fonts are web fonts hosted online that need to be downloaded by the browser but offer far more design variety.
