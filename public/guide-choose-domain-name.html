Why Your Domain Name Matters

Your domain name is your identity on the web. Just like a physical storefront, a good domain name creates a strong first impression, builds credibility, and helps people find you easily. It's the foundation of your brand's digital presence.

Pro Tip: Changing a domain name later can be complicated and negatively impact your SEO. It's worth taking the time to choose the right one from the start.

7 Rules for Choosing the Perfect Domain

1. Keep It Short and Simple

Aim for a domain name that is under 15 characters if possible. Long domains are harder to remember and easier to mistype. Avoid complex words that people might struggle to spell.

2. Make It Memorable and Brandable

Your domain should stand out. "Brandable" means that when you hear the name, it sounds like a real business rather than just a generic keyword string. Think google.com versus bestsearchengine.com.

3. Avoid Numbers and Hyphens

Numbers and hyphens cause confusion. If your domain is number1-web-design.com, people won't know if they should type the number "1" or spell out "one", and they frequently forget the hyphens.

4. Choose the Right Extension (.com is Still King)

While there are hundreds of new extensions (TLDs) available like .tech, .store, and .app, the .com extension remains the most recognized and trusted by users. Always try to get the .com first. If you're a local business targeting a specific country, a country-code TLD (like .uk or .ca) is also a great choice.

5. Check for Trademarks

Before you register a domain, ensure you aren't infringing on an existing trademark. A quick search of the trademark database in your country can save you from a costly legal dispute and the loss of your domain.

6. Think Long-Term

Don't pigeonhole yourself. If you sell shoes and choose bestrunning-shoes.com, you might struggle to expand into selling athletic apparel later. Pick a name broad enough to allow your business to grow.

7. Act Fast

Domain names sell quickly. If you find a name you love and it's available, register it immediately. Many registrars offer domains for a very low initial price, so the risk is minimal.
