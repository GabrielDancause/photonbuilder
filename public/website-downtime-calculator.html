Frequently Asked Questions

What does five nines (99.999%) uptime mean?
Five nines (99.999%) uptime is a highly sought-after standard for high availability systems. It means a service is guaranteed to be unavailable for no more than 5.26 minutes per year, or just about 864 milliseconds per day.
How is website downtime calculated?
Website downtime is calculated by taking the total time in a given period (e.g., 24 hours in a day, or 365 days in a year), multiplying it by the downtime percentage (100% minus the uptime percentage), and converting the result into human-readable units like hours, minutes, and seconds.
What is a good uptime percentage for a website?
For most standard websites, 99.9% (three nines) is considered a good uptime percentage. This translates to about 8.76 hours of downtime per year. Mission-critical systems often aim for 99.99% or 99.999% uptime.
Does scheduled maintenance count as downtime?
In Service Level Agreements (SLAs), scheduled maintenance is typically excluded from downtime calculations, provided it occurs within specified maintenance windows and customers are notified in advance. Unplanned outages are what usually count against uptime guarantees.
Why does website downtime matter?
Website downtime matters because it directly impacts revenue, brand reputation, user trust, and search engine rankings (SEO). Extended outages can frustrate customers and drive them to competitors.
