Growth Trends & Market Predictions (2026 Perspective)

WordPress Dominance

With an astounding 42.5% of the entire web running on WordPress, representing nearly 60% of all CMS deployments, its sheer volume creates an immense ecosystem. The challenge for competitors like Webflow and Wix is transitioning from niche visual building to capturing complex, enterprise-grade deployments that WordPress currently handles through its plugin ecosystem.

E-commerce Trajectories

Shopify holds a robust 5.1% of all websites, translating to a powerful 7.2% CMS market share. Its dedicated focus on commerce significantly outpaces general-purpose visual builders in specialized transactional environments. Expect Shopify to continue solidifying its lead in the dedicated e-commerce builder space.

The Visual Builder Tier

Wix (4.3% overall) and Squarespace (2.5% overall) form the middle tier of consumer and small-business website builders. They command a combined 9.5% of the CMS market. Their primary growth vector involves adding AI-driven generation tools to lower the barrier of entry for new businesses.

Developer-Centric Platforms

Webflow, despite strong mindshare among designers and agencies, represents 0.9% of all websites (1.2% CMS share). While its raw market share is smaller than Wix or Squarespace, its trajectory points toward high-value, bespoke builds that bridge the gap between custom coding and visual layout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which website builder has the largest market share in 2026?

WordPress continues to dominate the market in 2026, powering over 42.5% of all websites on the internet and holding nearly 60% of the Content Management System (CMS) market share.

Is Shopify better than Wix for e-commerce?

Yes, for dedicated e-commerce, Shopify is generally considered superior. It commands a 7.2% CMS market share specifically because of its robust, built-in payment processing, extensive e-commerce app store, and multichannel selling capabilities, whereas Wix is a more general-purpose visual builder.

What is the average cost of a website builder like Squarespace or Wix?

Average starting costs for mainstream visual builders like Wix and Squarespace range from $16 to $23 per month for basic plans, scaling up to $150+ for advanced commerce or enterprise features.

Why is Webflow popular among designers despite a smaller market share?

Webflow holds about 1.2% of the CMS market share but is highly favored by designers and agencies because it offers a visual coding interface that bridges the gap between custom coding and drag-and-drop layouts, allowing for complex animations and clean code export.

Are visual website builders growing faster than traditional CMS platforms?

Visual builders like Wix and Squarespace are seeing strong growth in the small-business sector, particularly by integrating AI-driven generation tools. However, traditional CMS platforms like WordPress still maintain an insurmountable volume lead for complex and enterprise-grade deployments.
