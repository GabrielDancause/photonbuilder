Pricing

Tier 2: Build-Your-Own / Developer Platforms

Pricing
Developer French: High Quality

Verdict for Auberge de nos Aïeux

For a 40+ room hotel in Charlevoix where 80-90% of calls are in Quebec French, voice quality and accent localization are critical to maintaining hospitality standards.

Option A: The Pragmatic Choice (Goodcall)

If you don't have a developer and need something set up quickly to handle basic FAQs (parking, directions, restaurant hours) and take messages, Goodcall is the most predictable ($66-$108/mo). However, you must test their French voice thoroughly to ensure it sounds acceptable to Quebecois callers, as they don't offer the deep voice customization of developer platforms.

Option B: The Hospitality Standard (Synthflow.ai or Vapi.ai)

If you want to integrate with your hotel's PMS to answer "Do you have rooms this weekend?" or if you want a perfect, natural-sounding Quebecois accent (via ElevenLabs), use a platform like Synthflow or Vapi.ai. Synthflow offers a slightly easier learning curve than Vapi thanks to its visual builder. Estimated cost is ~$75-$90/month for your volume, but requires initial setup by a tech-savvy person or agency to configure the workflows, prompts, and API connections.

Recommendation: Test Synthflow or Vapi using an ElevenLabs French-Canadian voice. The slight increase in setup complexity pays off massively in how naturally it interacts with your French-speaking guests, preserving the Auberge's welcoming atmosphere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI phone agents understand Quebec French?
Yes, many modern AI voice agents use models like ElevenLabs or OpenAI TTS/STT, which can understand and speak Quebec French (fr-CA) reasonably well. Voice quality and naturalness depend on the provider, with ElevenLabs offering high-quality, customizable options.
How much does an AI phone answering service cost for a small hotel?
For ~200 calls a month (assuming 2-3 minutes per call), ready-to-use platforms like Goodcall charge around $66-$108/month. Usage-based platforms like Vapi or Synthflow cost roughly $0.07-$0.14 per minute, totaling $40-$85/month, depending on the models used (TTS, LLM, Telephony).
Can an AI phone agent integrate with a hotel PMS?
Yes, but the depth of integration varies. Developer-focused platforms like Vapi, Synthflow, and Retell support webhooks, APIs, and custom functions, allowing them to check availability or pull pricing directly from a PMS. Simpler tools rely on Zapier or generic workflows.
Can the AI transfer calls to a human receptionist?
Yes, virtually all platforms (including Goodcall, Dialzara, Vapi, and Retell) support call forwarding or transferring to human staff when the AI is unable to answer a question, the caller requests to speak to a human, or outside of specific business hours.
Is it better to use a ready-made service or build my own?
For a 40-room hotel like Auberge de nos Aïeux, a ready-made platform is easier to set up but may lack specific Quebec French nuance or deep integration. Building on top of a low-code/API platform like Synthflow or Vapi offers much better voice quality and custom hotel interactions if you have the technical resources to set it up.
