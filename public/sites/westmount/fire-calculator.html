Methodology

This calculator estimates the time required to achieve Financial Independence based on the principles of the FIRE movement. The core assumption is that you achieve financial independence when your investment portfolio can generate enough passive income to cover your living expenses indefinitely.

  • FIRE Number: Calculated as Annual Expenses / Safe Withdrawal Rate. For example, $50,000 / 0.04 = $1,250,000.
  • Growth Calculation: Portfolio growth is compounded annually. Each year, your current savings grow by the Expected Annual Return, and your annual savings (Income - Expenses) are added to the portfolio.
  • Inflation: By using a "real" (inflation-adjusted) return rate—typically 5-7% rather than the nominal 9-10%—the calculations account for inflation, meaning the final target number is in today's dollars.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "4% Rule"?

The 4% rule is a rule of thumb based on the Trinity Study, which found that a portfolio consisting of 50% stocks and 50% bonds could sustain a 4% inflation-adjusted withdrawal rate for 30 years with a very high success rate. Many in the FIRE community use it to determine their target portfolio size.

Should I use pre-tax or post-tax income?

You should use post-tax (take-home) income. This represents the actual cash you have available to either spend or save.

How do taxes in retirement factor in?

Your "Annual Expenses" figure should include your estimated taxes in retirement. If you plan to spend $40,000 on living expenses but will owe $5,000 in taxes, your total Annual Expenses should be $45,000.
