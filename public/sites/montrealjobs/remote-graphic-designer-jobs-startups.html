💡 How to Search for Startup Roles

Landing a design role at a Canadian tech company requires a different approach than traditional agency or corporate jobs.

1. Rethink Your Portfolio

  • Startups don't just want pretty pictures; they want to see impact.
  • Frame your case studies around metrics: "Increased conversion by X%" or "Reduced time-to-market by Y".
  • Include a mix of high-fidelity brand work and gritty, fast-turnaround growth marketing assets.

2. Master the Tech Stack

  • Figma is non-negotiable. Period.
  • Show proficiency in AI tools (Midjourney, Firefly) to demonstrate you can scale output.
  • Familiarity with tools like Webflow, Framer, or basic HTML/CSS makes you significantly more valuable to a lean team.

3. Where to Look (Besides Here)

  • Avoid generic job boards like Indeed.
  • Check Wellfound (formerly AngelList), BuiltIn, and Otta.
  • Look at portfolios of Canadian VC firms (Inovia, Real Ventures, OMERS Ventures) and check their portfolio companies' career pages directly.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical salary for a remote graphic designer at a Canadian startup?

Salaries typically range from $65,000 CAD for junior/mid-level roles to over $110,000 CAD for senior positions. This often doesn't include equity (stock options) which is standard at Series B+ startups.

Do Canadian startups hire remote workers outside of their province?

Yes, many Canadian tech companies like Shopify, Wealthsimple, and 1Password have embraced a "Remote First" or "Work from Anywhere in Canada" model, allowing them to hire the best talent regardless of location.

What skills are most in-demand for startup designers right now?

Beyond strong visual fundamentals, startups are looking for designers who understand Figma, AI generative tools (Midjourney, Firefly), basic motion design, and who can work fast in a growth-focused marketing environment.

Is equity common for design roles in Canadian startups?

Yes, it is very common. Early-stage startups (Seed/Series A) often offer larger equity grants with lower base salaries, while later-stage companies (Series C+) offer competitive salaries along with standard equity packages (RSUs or options).
