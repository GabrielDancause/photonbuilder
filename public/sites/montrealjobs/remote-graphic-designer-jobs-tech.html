Remote Graphic Designer Jobs at Tech Companies

High-paying remote and hybrid brand design roles across Canada's tech sector.

📅 Last updated: July 2025 • Listings updated periodically

Working as a graphic designer in the tech sector offers a massive shift in compensation, work-life balance, and remote flexibility compared to traditional print shops or advertising agencies. Canadian tech companies—from unicorns like Shopify to rapidly growing startups—are aggressively hiring visual communicators to elevate their brand presence.

The roles often go by titles like Brand Designer, Visual Designer, or Communication Designer, but the core foundation remains graphic design. Below is our curated list of representative opportunities currently (or frequently) open across the Canadian tech landscape.

💡 Why target tech companies?

  • Superior Compensation: Base salaries typically range from $75,000 to $120,000+ CAD.
  • Equity: Many tech companies offer stock options (RSUs) as part of the total compensation package.
  • Remote First: The tech industry leads in offering permanent remote or flexible hybrid work structures, meaning you can live in Montreal, the South Shore, or anywhere else.

🔍 How to Search for These Roles

Tech companies often use different terminology than traditional design agencies. If you are searching on LinkedIn or Indeed, using the term "Graphic Designer" might limit your results to print shops or traditional corporate roles.

🎯 The Tech Design Lexicon

Expand your search using these terms:

  • Brand Designer / Visual Designer: The tech equivalents of a senior graphic designer. Focuses heavily on digital presence, marketing websites, and visual identity.
  • Communication Designer: Focuses on presentation design, sales enablement, internal communications, and marketing collateral.
  • Digital Designer: Often heavily focused on performance marketing (ads), social media, and email campaigns.
  • Web Designer: While sometimes leaning into UX/UI, in marketing departments, this role is focused on designing visually stunning landing pages.

🛠️ Practical Tips for Transitioning to Tech

  1. Learn Figma: Even if you aren't doing UI design, Figma is the standard collaborative tool in tech. You will be expected to design marketing sites, social assets, and presentations in Figma.
  2. Understand the Business: Tech companies value designers who understand metrics. Learn basic concepts like Conversion Rate (CR), Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), and A/B testing.
  3. Update Your Portfolio: Remove print-heavy projects (unless they are part of a massive brand identity). Focus on responsive web design, digital campaigns, motion/video, and social media ecosystems.
  4. Leverage Remote: Don't limit yourself to local Montreal companies. Search for remote roles in Toronto, Vancouver, or US companies hiring remotely in Canada (which often pay even more).

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do Canadian tech companies pay graphic designers well?

Yes. While traditional design agencies often pay $45,000–$65,000 CAD, tech companies (like Shopify, Wealthsimple, or 1Password) typically offer much higher compensation. Mid-level graphic designers at these companies often see salaries ranging from $75,000 to $110,000 CAD, alongside stock options (RSUs or ISOs) and strong benefits.

Is remote work still common for designers in tech?

Yes, especially in Canada. Many top Canadian tech companies have adopted 'digital by default' or highly flexible hybrid models. This means you can often work from anywhere in Canada (including Montreal or the South Shore) while earning a top-tier salary.

What skills do tech companies look for beyond just visual design?

Tech companies heavily value cross-functional collaboration. You'll need to demonstrate experience working with marketing teams, copywriters, and sometimes product designers. Proficiency in Figma, understanding of web accessibility, animation/motion design (After Effects), and basic understanding of conversion rate optimization (CRO) are massive advantages.

How is a "Brand Designer" different from a "Graphic Designer" in tech?

In the tech sector, "Brand Designer" is often the modern evolution of the graphic designer role. Brand designers focus on holistic visual identity, marketing websites, campaign assets, and design systems, whereas product designers (UX/UI) focus on the software application itself. Transitioning your title to Brand Designer can often unlock higher salary bands.
