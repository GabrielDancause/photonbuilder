Salary Guide & Roles (CAD)

Compensation in Montreal has steadily increased, driven by fierce competition among top studios and the influx of foreign capital. While salaries may appear lower than San Francisco in pure USD conversion, the cost of living and robust healthcare system offer a highly competitive quality of life.

Technical Artist (Tech Art)
$85,000 - $140,000+

The bridge between art and programming. Highly sought after. Requires deep knowledge of Houdini, Unreal Engine Blueprints/C++, and rendering pipelines.

3D Environment/Character Artist
$65,000 - $110,000

Requires mastery of ZBrush, Maya/Blender, and Substance. Senior environment artists who can optimize for current-gen consoles command the upper end.

UI/UX Designer
$70,000 - $115,000

Designing HUDs, menus, and player onboarding. A strong transition path for traditional digital/web designers. Figma to Unreal UMG pipelines are standard.

Game Designer
$65,000 - $120,000

System, level, and economy designers. Relies heavily on rapid prototyping skills. Senior economy designers for live-service games can push $130k+.

Concept Artist
$60,000 - $100,000

Highly competitive. Studios now expect proficiency in integrating 3D blockouts (Blender) and AI iteration tools alongside traditional Photoshop painting.

DevOps / Build Engineer
$90,000 - $150,000+

Managing CI/CD pipelines, Perforce servers, and cloud infrastructure. Often the highest-paid non-management individual contributors.

QA Analyst
$45,000 - $65,000

The traditional entry point. Moving into QA Automation (Python, C#) significantly boosts salary trajectories.

The Secret Sauce: The CDAE Tax Credit

Why are there so many studios in Montreal? The answer is the Credit d'impôt pour le développement des affaires électroniques (CDAE), often referred to as the Multimedia Title Tax Credit.

The Quebec government provides a refundable tax credit that subsidizes up to 37.5% of eligible labor costs for game development. This means if a studio pays an employee $100,000, the government essentially refunds the studio $37,500. This massive financial lever makes it incredibly cost-effective for global publishers to place their largest, most labor-intensive AAA development teams in the province.

Transitioning from Graphic Design to Games

For traditional graphic designers, advertising creatives, and web designers looking to break into the Montreal games industry, direct paths exist, but require specific portfolio pivots:

  • UI/UX Design: The most direct translation of web/app design skills. You must learn node-based logic (Unreal UMG, Unity UI Toolkit) and demonstrate how your interfaces respond to controller/gamepad inputs.
  • Marketing Art: Studios need high-end key art, social media assets, and trailer graphics. Strong typography, compositing, and branding skills are highly valued here.
  • Concept Art: A harder pivot. Requires transitioning from design logic to pure illustration, draftsmanship, and mood-setting. Incorporating 3D bases (Blender) and AI iteration into your workflow is now mandatory to keep up with production speeds.

Networking Tip: Attend the Montreal International Games Summit (MIGS) or local IGDA chapter meetups. The industry is highly concentrated—who you know often dictates how fast your portfolio gets reviewed.
