🚨 Market Reality for Packaging Designers

Unlike digital product design, packaging design (print, structural, CPG) requires rigorous technical knowledge of prepress, materials, and die-lines. Because physical proofs are often needed, pure 100% remote roles are competitive. However, post-2020, many top Canadian agencies and brands have adapted by mailing proofs to remote designers.

🔍 How to Search for These Roles

Packaging design is a niche field. If you simply search for "Graphic Designer," you will be flooded with digital and social media roles. Use these targeted strategies:

Prepress Technical Print Skills

Key Search Terms

  • Job Titles: Packaging Designer, Packaging Art Director, Structural Designer, Prepress Production Artist, CPG Designer, Label Designer.
  • Skill Keywords: Die-lines, Flexography, Lithography, Esko, ArtiosCAD, Corrugated, Spot Colors, FDA/Health Canada compliance, Adobe Dimension.

Agencies vs. In-House

Agencies: Look for specialized branding and packaging agencies like Pigeon Brands, Shikatani Lacroix, Davis, or LG2. These roles are fast-paced, involve diverse clients, and focus heavily on conceptual design.

In-House: Target major Canadian CPG companies (Lululemon, Aldo, Saputo, Olymel, Canada Goose, Deciem). These roles offer deep involvement in a single brand's ecosystem and often provide better work-life balance.

Pro Tip: The "Production Artist" Route

If you are struggling to land a Senior Conceptual Packaging Designer role, look for "Prepress" or "Production Artist" roles at packaging agencies. These roles teach you the rigorous technical side of how files are actually printed (trapping, color separation). It is the best stepping stone to becoming an elite packaging designer.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average salary for a remote packaging designer in Canada?

In Canada, the average salary for a remote packaging designer ranges from $60,000 to $85,000 CAD per year. Senior positions at major CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods) brands or specialized design agencies can exceed $95,000 CAD, while junior or intermediate roles typically start around $50,000 to $65,000 CAD.

What software skills are strictly required for packaging design?

Adobe Illustrator is non-negotiable, particularly proficiency with die-lines, bleeds, and vector art. Adobe Photoshop is essential for mockups and photo retouching. Increasingly, 3D software like Adobe Dimension, Cinema 4D, or Blender is requested for rendering realistic packaging concepts before physical prototyping.

Is it possible to find 100% remote packaging design jobs?

Yes, but they are less common than general graphic design roles because packaging often requires evaluating physical printed samples, materials, and structural integrity. However, many Canadian agencies and CPG companies now offer fully remote or hybrid roles where physical proofs are mailed directly to the designer's home office.

What should I include in a packaging design portfolio?

Your portfolio must show the entire process: initial concepts, flat die-lines with technical callouts, high-quality 3D renders, and photographs of the final printed product on shelves if possible. Demonstrating an understanding of print production (spot colors, varnishes, sustainable materials) is crucial.
