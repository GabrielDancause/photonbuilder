🎯 How to Search for Motion Roles in Canada

1. Your Reel is Everything

In motion design, nobody reads your resume until they've watched your reel. Keep it under 60 seconds. Start with your absolute best work. Cut ruthlessly—one mediocre shot can ruin the impression of ten great ones. Host it on Vimeo or build a clean portfolio site; do not send Google Drive links to video files.

2. Look Beyond "Agencies"

While traditional ad agencies (LG2, Cossette, Rethink) hire motion designers, the fastest-growing sectors are:

  • Tech & SaaS (Remote): Companies need motion for product explainers, social ads, and UI micro-interactions. (e.g., Shopify, Wealthsimple, 1Password)
  • Gaming & Web3: Montreal and Vancouver are hubs for game studios that need motion graphics for UI, trailers, and marketing. (e.g., EA, Ubisoft, Behaviour Interactive)
  • Experiential: Montreal is a global leader in this space. (e.g., Moment Factory, Normal Studio)

3. The "Product Motion" Niche

A highly lucrative niche is bridging the gap between UX/UI and Motion. If you understand how to export animations using Lottie (Bodymovin) or Rive for developers to implement directly into apps and websites, your value to tech companies skyrockets. Learn Figma to AE workflows.

4. Where to Actually Look

  • LinkedIn: Set alerts for "Motion Designer", "Animator", and "Video Editor" (often poorly titled). Set location to "Canada" with the "Remote" filter checked.
  • Behance & Dribbble Jobs: Surprisingly active for remote design roles.
  • Motionographer Jobs Board: Industry standard, though skewed heavily towards US/Global roles.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a remote motion designer make in Canada?

Salaries for remote motion designers in Canada typically range from $60,000 to $85,000 CAD for mid-level roles, while senior motion designers and art directors can earn between $90,000 and $120,000+ CAD. Rates vary heavily based on whether you work for a local agency or a US-based tech company hiring remotely in Canada.

What software do I need to know for motion design jobs?

After Effects is the non-negotiable industry standard for 2D motion design. For 3D roles, Cinema 4D is highly preferred, often alongside Redshift or Octane renderers. Knowledge of Premiere Pro, Illustrator, and Photoshop is expected. Figma is becoming increasingly important for product-focused motion design.

Are there many fully remote motion design jobs in Canada?

Yes, motion design is highly conducive to remote work. While many traditional advertising agencies have moved to hybrid models (2-3 days in office), tech companies, gaming studios, and specialized production houses frequently offer fully remote roles for candidates based anywhere in Canada.

Do I need a formal degree to become a motion designer?

No. In motion design, your showreel is your resume. While a degree in Graphic Design, Animation, or Film can be helpful for foundational knowledge, employers overwhelmingly prioritize the quality of your portfolio and your technical execution over formal education.
