Montreal Creative Industry Salary Study

The Definitive 2026 Compensation Guide

Published: March 2026 • Currency: CAD

As Montreal cements itself as a North American hub for creativity, technology, and artificial intelligence, the compensation landscape is shifting rapidly. This 2026 Salary Study aggregates data from thousands of job listings, industry surveys, and self-reported figures to provide the most accurate, realistic view of the creative and tech markets in Montreal today.

Our methodology aggregates verified data from Robert Half, Glassdoor, the Canada Job Bank, and real-time LinkedIn Salary Insights, cross-referenced with local recruiter data to normalize anomalies. We highlight base salaries in Canadian Dollars (CAD) exclusive of bonuses, equity, or benefits.

🚀 The 2026 AI Premium

In 2026, we are seeing a structural shift in compensation based on tooling proficiency. Roles that traditionally required pure execution now demand integration of Generative AI tools (Midjourney, Copilot, Cursor, etc.). Candidates who demonstrably utilize AI to achieve a 3-5x productivity multiplier are commanding a 15-25% salary premium over baseline market rates, across all experience levels.

🏢 Impact by Company Type

Where you work in Montreal dictates your compensation structure as much as what you do. The ecosystem is heavily segmented:

Creative Agencies -10%

Lower base, but unparalleled portfolio growth. High burnout rate.

Startups (Seed/Series A) Median

Average base, heavily subsidized by high-risk equity (stock options).

Enterprise / Big Tech +20%

Top quartile pay, excellent benefits, slower promotion cycles.

Slightly lower base, offset by exceptional pensions and job security.

The Freelance Market (2026)

Montreal's freelance market remains robust, particularly for AI-enabled creatives who can turn around assets at high speed. Typical hourly rates for independent contractors:

  • Graphic Design: $45 - $80 / hr
  • UX/UI Design: $65 - $120 / hr
  • Motion Design: $70 - $110 / hr
  • Web Development: $75 - $130 / hr
  • DevOps Consulting: $100 - $175+ / hr

Tip: Freelancers who successfully transition to project-based pricing (value-based) rather than hourly tracking report 40-60% higher effective annual incomes.

🌎 Regional Comparison: Montreal vs. The Rest of Canada

Montreal has historically offered lower salaries offset by a drastically lower cost of living. However, in 2026, the gap has narrowed. Real estate and rent in Montreal have surged, while salaries have only partially caught up. Montreal remains cheaper than Toronto or Vancouver, but the "Montreal Discount" on salaries is a point of friction for local talent.
