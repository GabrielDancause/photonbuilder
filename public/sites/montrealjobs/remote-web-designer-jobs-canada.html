🎯 How to Search for Web Design Roles

The Shift Toward "No-Code"

In 2025, the line between "Web Designer" and "Web Developer" is blurring. Canadian agencies and tech companies increasingly expect web designers to implement their own designs using visual development platforms like Webflow, Framer, and advanced Squarespace environments. Highlighting these tools in your portfolio significantly boosts your hireability.

Keyword Strategies

When searching job boards like Indeed Canada or LinkedIn, "Web Designer" can sometimes yield broad or low-quality results. Try these targeted variations:

  • For No-Code roles: "Webflow Designer", "Framer Designer", "Visual Developer"
  • For Marketing/Agency roles: "Digital Designer", "Interactive Designer", "Growth Designer"
  • For Product-adjacent roles: "UI Designer (Web)", "Web Experience Designer"

Portfolio Best Practices

  • Show, Don't Just Tell: If you are applying for a Webflow role, your portfolio should be built in Webflow. It serves as live proof of your technical ability.
  • Explain the 'Why': Don't just show beautiful mockups. Explain the business problem, the target audience, and how your design solved the issue.
  • Performance Matters: Ensure the sites you link to load quickly and are fully responsive. Employers will check your portfolio on their phones.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average salary for a remote web designer in Canada?

In Canada, a remote web designer's salary typically ranges from $60,000 to $95,000 CAD per year, depending on experience, specialization (like Webflow or custom React/Vue design), and the specific employer. Senior roles at major tech companies can exceed $120,000 CAD.

Do I need to know how to code to be a web designer?

Not necessarily. Many roles focus strictly on visual design (UI/UX) using tools like Figma or Adobe XD. However, "no-code" platforms like Webflow and Squarespace are increasingly popular, and understanding basic HTML/CSS is highly beneficial and often expected in modern roles.

Are Canadian companies hiring for remote design roles?

Yes, many Canadian tech companies and agencies, such as Shopify, Unbounce, Wealthsimple, and Jobber, frequently hire for remote or hybrid design positions across the country. The shift to remote work has made it easier for designers to work for companies outside their immediate geographical area.

What portfolio platform is best for web designers?

Building a custom portfolio on Webflow or Framer is highly recommended for web designers as it showcases your technical skills alongside your visual design capabilities. Traditional platforms like Behance or Dribbble are also good for individual case studies, but having your own domain is crucial.
