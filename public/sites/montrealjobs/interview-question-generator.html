How to Ace a Montreal Design Interview

Interviewing for a design role in Montreal requires a specific approach. The city's unique blend of North American business practices and European creative sensibilities means employers are looking for a specific type of candidate.

🗣️ The Bilingual Reality

Even if a job posting is in English, expect to hear French during the interview process. A common Montreal interview tactic is to suddenly switch languages mid-conversation to test your fluency and adaptability. If you are not fully bilingual, honesty is the best policy. Say: "Je comprends bien le français, mais je suis plus à l'aise pour expliquer mon processus créatif en anglais." (I understand French well, but I am more comfortable explaining my creative process in English.)

1. Portfolio Presentation (Le Portfolio)

Montreal agencies value storytelling as much as the final visual output. When presenting your portfolio:

  • Focus on the problem: Don't just show the pretty result. Explain the business problem you were trying to solve.
  • Show your process: Include sketches, wireframes, or failed iterations. Montreal creative directors love to see how you think, not just how you execute.
  • Highlight local context: If you have worked on campaigns for local brands (e.g., SAQ, Desjardins, local festivals), highlight them. Local cultural context is highly valued.

2. AI and Technology Integration

As highlighted in our market research, the integration of AI tools is becoming a key differentiator. Be prepared to discuss:

  • How you use tools like Midjourney or DALL-E for rapid ideation.
  • Your perspective on the ethical use of AI in commercial design.
  • How AI has improved your workflow efficiency, not replaced your creative input.

Pro Tip: Don't wait for them to ask about AI. Proactively bring it up when discussing your process. It shows forward-thinking and initiative.

3. Cultural Fit and Soft Skills

Montreal has a vibrant, tight-knit design community. Agencies here deeply care about team dynamics and "fit."

  • The "Beer Test": Many interviews are subtly evaluating if they would want to grab a drink with you after work at a local microbrewery.
  • Collaboration over Ego: Emphasize your ability to take constructive feedback and collaborate with developers, copywriters, and account managers.
  • Adaptability: Agency life in Montreal can be fast-paced with tight budgets. Show that you can be resourceful and agile.

4. Salary Expectations

Be prepared for the salary question. Use our salary data to ensure you are asking for a competitive rate. Remember that Montreal salaries are generally lower than Toronto or Vancouver, but the cost of living (while rising) is also historically lower. Always state your expectations in CAD.

🏢 Knowing Your Audience

Tailor your approach based on the company type:

  • Agencies (e.g., LG2, Cossette, Sid Lee): Expect intense portfolio scrutiny, questions about handling high-pressure deadlines, and a strong focus on creative vision.
  • Tech Startups (e.g., Hopper, Lightspeed): Focus on UX/UI, agile methodology, data-driven design decisions, and adaptability.
  • Enterprise (e.g., Bell, National Bank): Emphasize your ability to work within strict brand guidelines, stakeholder management, and accessibility standards.
