🎯 How to Search for These Roles

The gaming industry has its own specific language and expectations when it comes to hiring designers. A generic graphic design portfolio rarely cuts it. Here is how to position yourself to land a remote role at a Canadian game studio:

1. Understand the Divide: Marketing Art vs. In-Game Art

Marketing/Brand Designers: You will be creating key art, social media posts, logos, pitch decks, and app store screenshots. Your portfolio should look like an entertainment or movie marketing agency's site. Show that you can take 3D renders from the game and composite them into stunning posters.

UI/UX and 2D In-Game Artists: You are designing the actual game interface, HUDs, menus, icons, or 2D sprites. Your portfolio needs to show complex systems, states (hover, active, disabled), iconography, and a deep understanding of user flow.

2. Master the Tools

  • The Basics: Photoshop, Illustrator, and Figma are non-negotiable.
  • The Differentiators: Motion graphics (After Effects) is heavily requested for marketing roles to create animated social posts. For UI roles, familiarity with game engines (Unity, Unreal, Frostbite) or UI middleware will put you ahead of 90% of applicants.
  • AI Tools: As of 2025, tools like Midjourney and Adobe Firefly are increasingly used for rapid concepting and mood boards. Mentioning these workflows can be an asset.

3. Optimize Your Job Searches

Don't just search for "Graphic Designer". Game studios use specific nomenclature. Use these exact search strings on platforms like Indeed Canada, LinkedIn, and remote-specific boards:

  • "Marketing Artist" OR "Marketing Graphic Designer" AND game
  • "UI Artist" OR "User Interface Artist" AND studio
  • "2D Artist" OR "Concept Artist" AND remote AND Canada
  • "Brand Designer" AND entertainment OR gaming

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do Canadian game studios hire fully remote graphic designers?
Yes. Since 2020, major studios like Behaviour Interactive, Phoenix Labs, and Klei Entertainment have maintained robust remote work policies. Many studios hire anywhere within Canada to tap into the nationwide talent pool, though some require you to reside in specific provinces (like Quebec or BC) for tax credit purposes.
What is the average salary for a remote graphic designer in the Canadian gaming industry?
In Canada, a mid-level remote graphic designer in gaming typically earns between $60,000 and $85,000 CAD. Senior designers and specialized 2D artists can earn between $90,000 and $130,000 CAD, depending on the studio size and project scope. Performance marketing designers at mobile studios can also earn lucrative bonuses.
What portfolio pieces do game studios look for in a graphic designer?
Game studios look for highly polished marketing art, key art, logo design, typography tailored to specific game genres, UI/UX mockups, and social media assets. They want to see that you understand how to translate a game's core visual identity into compelling promotional materials, rather than just standard corporate branding.
Is French required for remote roles at Montreal-based studios?
It depends. If the studio is based in Quebec (like Ubisoft Montreal or Behaviour) and the role involves internal communication with local teams, bilingualism (French/English) is often required or strongly preferred. However, many studios hiring remotely across Canada, or global studios with Canadian branches, operate primarily in English.
