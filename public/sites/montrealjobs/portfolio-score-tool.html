Understanding the Montreal Market (2026)

A portfolio that wowed employers in 2023 might get filtered out today. The integration of generative AI, the shift towards full-stack product design, and tight agency budgets mean your portfolio must work harder than ever. Here is exactly what different types of employers are looking for right now.

The Montreal Agency Market

Agencies like Sid Lee, LG2, and Cossette are looking for velocity and versatility. They operate in a bilingual, fast-paced environment where margins matter.

  • Velocity Demonstration: They want to see how fast you work. Case studies that show your timeline (e.g., "From brief to delivery in 4 days") are highly prized.
  • The "AI Multiplier": Agencies love designers who can use Midjourney or Firefly to bypass expensive stock photo licensing or speed up storyboarding. Show how your AI use saves client budget.
  • Bilingual Assets: Even if you are an English speaker, your portfolio should demonstrate the ability to handle bilingual typographic layouts seamlessly. French and English text lengths differ significantly; show you know how to handle it.
  • Pitch Readiness: Can you design beautiful pitch decks? Including a highly polished presentation template in your portfolio often seals the deal for junior/mid-level agency hires. Average agency salaries for mid-level designers in Montreal sit around $65,000 - $75,000 CAD.

What Startups Look For

Montreal's thriving tech scene (AI startups, SaaS companies around Mile End and Downtown) hires differently than agencies. They want "Full-Stack" designers.

  • The "0 to 1" Experience: Startups want to see a product you designed from absolute scratch. They care less about perfect branding and more about user acquisition, onboarding flows, and functional UI.
  • Systems Thinking: You must demonstrate you can build and maintain a scalable design system in Figma using variables, auto-layout, and advanced components.
  • Conversion Focus: How did your design impact the bottom line? A case study stating "Increased signup conversion by 14%" is worth five case studies with no data.
  • Speed over Polish: Startups value iterative design. Show your ugly wireframes and how you quickly tested and refined them. Startups generally pay a premium, with intermediate product designers earning $80,000 - $110,000 CAD, plus equity.

The Enterprise Reality

Banks (Desjardins, National Bank), telecom (Bell, Videotron), and large corporations (CGI, CAE) look for stability, accessibility, and collaboration.

  • Accessibility (A11y): Your portfolio must explicitly mention WCAG standards. Show that you test for contrast, screen reader compatibility, and keyboard navigation. This is a hard requirement for enterprise roles.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Case studies must highlight how you worked with developers, PMs, and stakeholders. Describe how you handled disagreements or technical constraints.
  • Complexity Management: Show that you can take a highly complex enterprise workflow (like a banking dashboard with 50 data points) and make it intuitive.
  • Compliance & Security UX: If you have examples of designing secure, compliant flows without frustrating the user, highlight them. Enterprise roles offer great stability, pensions, and salaries typically ranging from $75,000 - $95,000 CAD.

The 2026 AI Portfolio Checklist

We analyzed over 200 job postings in the Greater Montreal Area. Employers rarely list "Prompt Engineer" as a job title for designers, but they consistently hire the designer who demonstrates AI workflow integration over the one who doesn't. Here is what you must include to show AI proficiency.

To be competitive in 2026, ensure your portfolio includes:

  • Generative Ideation Showcase: Include a section in at least one case study showing 10-20 AI-generated concepts (Midjourney/DALL-E) that you used as a mood board before creating the final manual design.
  • Workflow Automation Proof: A brief write-up or video demonstrating how you use tools like Figma AI, Relume, or ChatGPT to automate tedious tasks (e.g., generating realistic placeholder data, translating copy, structuring initial wireframes).
  • Image Extension & Repair: Show a clear "Before and After" demonstrating your use of Photoshop Generative Fill to extend backgrounds, remove complex elements, or alter lighting in client photos.
  • AI Asset Polish: Show that you understand AI output isn't final. Display an AI-generated asset, and then explicitly point out the manual Photoshop/Illustrator work you did to fix artifacts, correct typography, and align it with brand guidelines.
  • Custom Model/LoRA (Bonus): The holy grail of the 2026 portfolio. If you can show that you trained a specific AI model on a client's brand guidelines to generate on-brand assets consistently, you move to the top 1% of candidates.

Why this matters

As discussed in our Market Overview, South Shore and Montreal companies are looking to do more with less. A designer who can articulate "I used AI to cut down asset creation time by 40%, allowing me to spend more time on strategy and user testing" demonstrates business value, not just technical skill. When applying to companies like Olymel, Coveo, or local agencies, this framing is highly effective.
