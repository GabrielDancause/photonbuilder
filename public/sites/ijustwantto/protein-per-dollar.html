Methodology (2026)

To determine the true cost of protein, we analyzed retail pricing and nutritional data for 30+ common foods. Our methodology included:

  • Pricing Sources: Publicly available 2026 pricing data from major US retailers including Walmart and Instacart.
  • Nutritional Data: Verified against the USDA FoodData Central database.
  • Calculation: (Grams of Protein per Serving) ÷ (Price USD per Serving) = Grams of Protein per Dollar.
  • Standardization: We used standard servings or normalized to 100g where appropriate for raw meats.

Note: Prices fluctuate locally and seasonally. Use these figures as a relative baseline rather than an exact shopping list guarantee.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest source of protein?

Based on our 2026 data, dry legumes like lentils and dry black beans, along with whole chicken, consistently rank as the absolute cheapest sources of protein. You can get over 60g of protein per dollar with dry beans, though they do require more preparation time than other sources.

How much protein is in an egg?

One large egg contains approximately 6 grams of protein. While eggs are relatively inexpensive per unit, because they are lower in total protein compared to a serving of meat, their "protein per dollar" value usually sits firmly in the middle of the pack.

Is chicken breast the best protein source?

Chicken breast is an excellent lean protein source, providing around 31g of protein per 100g serving with very little fat. However, whole chickens and chicken thighs often provide better "protein per dollar" value if you are strictly optimizing for budget.

Are protein powders worth the money?

Yes. Despite the high upfront cost of a tub, whey protein powder is actually one of the most cost-effective protein sources available per serving. It frequently beats out many fresh meats in terms of protein per dollar, while requiring zero preparation.

What is the cheapest plant-based protein?

Dry beans (like black beans) and dry lentils are the cheapest plant-based proteins. Buying them dry rather than canned significantly decreases the cost per serving while yielding the same nutritional profile.
