How to Choose the Right Workout App

Finding the right fitness app depends heavily on your specific goals, workout environment, and preferred training style. For those focused on progressive overload and lifting weights in a gym setting, tracking apps like Strong or JEFIT are ideal. They replace the traditional pen-and-paper logbook, allowing you to easily track sets, reps, and rest periods while providing visual data on your progress over time.

Conversely, if you prefer working out at home with minimal equipment, guided video apps are likely a better fit. Nike Training Club (NTC) and FitOn offer studio-quality classes spanning HIIT, yoga, mobility, and bodyweight strength. NTC is particularly notable as it provides all its content completely free without any paywalls or advertisements.

Are Free Workout Apps Worth It?

Absolutely. The free tiers of the apps listed above provide more than enough functionality for the vast majority of users to achieve their fitness goals. While premium versions often unlock features like advanced analytics, casting to smart TVs, or unlimited custom routine slots, the core functionalities—access to workouts and basic tracking—are usually robust in the free versions.

  • Nike Training Club: 100% free forever. No premium tier exists, making it an incredible value.
  • Strong: The free version limits you to 3 custom routines, which is sufficient if you follow a standard Push/Pull/Legs or Full Body split.
  • FitOn & JEFIT: Both offer massive libraries of free content, with premium tiers mostly removing ads or offering personalized meal plans and advanced data insights.
