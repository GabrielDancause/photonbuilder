How the Running Pace Calculator Works

A running pace calculator uses a simple mathematical relationship between three variables: Distance, Time, and Pace. If you know any two of these variables, you can accurately calculate the third.

  • Pace = Time ÷ Distance
  • Time = Pace × Distance
  • Distance = Time ÷ Pace

This tool converts all inputs into a common unit (meters for distance and seconds for time) behind the scenes to ensure accurate calculations across kilometers and miles.

Understanding Race Predictions

The race predictions provided by this tool use Riegel's Formula, developed by engineer Peter Riegel in 1977. The formula is widely accepted as one of the most accurate ways to predict race times based on a recent performance.

The formula is: T2 = T1 × (D2 ÷ D1)^1.06, where T1 is the time achieved for distance D1, and T2 is the predicted time for distance D2. It assumes you have done the appropriate training for the target distance.

Why Calculate Your Splits?

A splits table breaks down your total race or run into smaller, manageable chunks (typically 1 kilometer or 1 mile). Knowing your splits is crucial for even pacing—the strategy used by almost all world-record breakers.

Starting a race too fast is a common mistake that leads to early fatigue. By generating a splits table, you can memorize or write down your target times for each marker, ensuring you stay on track and conserve energy for the finish.
