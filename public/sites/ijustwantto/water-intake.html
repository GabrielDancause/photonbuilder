How Much Water Should You Drink a Day?

Hydration is essential for nearly every function in the human body. While the classic "8 glasses a day" rule is easy to remember, it isn't accurate for everyone. Your actual water needs vary based on your weight, how active you are, and the climate you live in.

This calculator estimates a baseline of 0.5 ounces of water per pound of body weight. So, a person weighing 150 lbs would need about 75 ounces of water daily just resting in a normal climate.

Factors Affecting Hydration Needs

  • Exercise: When you sweat, you lose water quickly. We recommend adding 12 ounces of water to your daily total for every 30 minutes of exercise to replenish lost fluids.
  • Environment: Hot, humid, or extremely dry climates increase how much you perspire and how quickly moisture evaporates from your skin. You may need 15% to 30% more water in these conditions.
  • Health Conditions & Diet: Illnesses that cause fever, vomiting, or diarrhea increase water loss. Additionally, if you consume high-sodium foods, your body will require more water to process them.

Signs of Proper Hydration

The easiest way to tell if you're drinking enough water is by checking the color of your urine. A pale yellow or clear color typically indicates good hydration, while dark yellow or amber means you need to drink more water. Other signs of dehydration include fatigue, headaches, dry mouth, and dizziness.
