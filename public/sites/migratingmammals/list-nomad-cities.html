How We Rank Digital Nomad Cities

Choosing the right base as a digital nomad involves balancing multiple factors. We evaluate cities based on cost of living (rent, food, and coworking spaces), internet reliability, the presence of a strong nomad community, overall safety, and the availability of viable visa options.

The Importance of Visa Options in 2025

With many countries launching dedicated Digital Nomad Visas (DNVs), long-term stays have become significantly easier to navigate legally. Cities in countries like Spain, Portugal, Thailand, and Colombia now offer clear pathways for remote workers to stay from 6 months up to several years without relying on standard tourist visas or "visa runs."

Community vs. Cost

While affordability is often a primary driver for new nomads, community infrastructure is equally critical. Cities like Chiang Mai and Bali might have slightly rising costs compared to lesser-known alternatives, but they offer unparalleled networking opportunities, frequent meetups, and infrastructure perfectly tailored for remote professionals.
