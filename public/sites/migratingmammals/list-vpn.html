Why You Need a VPN While Traveling

As a digital nomad or frequent traveler, your data is constantly exposed. You connect to unfamiliar airport Wi-Fi, sketchy cafe networks, and hotel routers that may be compromised. A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your internet traffic, creating a secure tunnel between your device and the internet. This prevents bad actors, local ISPs, and even network administrators from seeing your passwords, browsing history, or intercepting sensitive work documents.

Beyond security, a VPN is essential for maintaining access to the tools and entertainment you rely on. Many banking apps, government portals, and corporate intranets will lock you out if they detect a login from an unusual foreign IP address. A VPN allows you to route your connection through your home country, bypassing these geographical security blocks.

How to Choose the Right VPN

Not all VPNs are created equal. When evaluating a VPN for travel, consider your primary use case. If you need to access geo-blocked streaming services like Netflix or BBC iPlayer, providers like NordVPN or ExpressVPN are your best bet due to their massive server networks and ability to evade VPN blockers.

If your primary concern is absolute privacy and anonymity—perhaps you are traveling to a region with heavy internet censorship—Mullvad is unmatched in its commitment to not collecting any user data whatsoever. Finally, if you travel with a large family or carry multiple devices (laptop, phone, tablet, e-reader), Surfshark's unlimited simultaneous connections offer the best value.
