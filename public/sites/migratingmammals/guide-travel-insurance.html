Travel Insurance vs. International Health Insurance

When selecting coverage as a digital nomad, it is crucial to understand the distinction between travel insurance and international health insurance.

Travel insurance (like World Nomads or SafetyWing's base plan) is designed for temporary trips. It covers emergency medical situations, but also includes travel-related mishaps like lost luggage, flight cancellations, and stolen electronics. It is not designed for routine checkups or long-term care.

International Health Insurance (like Genki Native or SafetyWing Nomad Health) functions more like the health insurance you have in your home country, but globally. It covers routine doctor visits, preventative care, and ongoing treatments, but typically offers zero coverage for travel logistics like lost bags or canceled flights.

How to Choose the Right Nomad Insurance

  • Assess Your Gear: If you travel with expensive laptops and cameras, a comprehensive plan that covers tech (like World Nomads) might be worth the premium. Alternatively, buy cheap medical-only insurance and insure your gear separately.
  • Consider Your Activities: Standard policies often exclude "extreme" sports. If you plan to ride a scooter in Southeast Asia, surf, or scuba dive, read the policy wording carefully. You usually need a specific license for scooters, and certain depth limits apply for diving.
  • Check Home Country Rules: Most nomads visit home occasionally. Check how many days of incidental home country coverage the policy provides, and whether coverage in the USA is included (it usually costs extra).

The Claim Process Reality

Regardless of the provider, travel insurance claims require meticulous documentation. If an item is stolen, you must get a police report within 24 hours. If you visit a doctor, you must keep all receipts, medical reports, and proof of payment. For major hospitalizations, always call the emergency assistance number on your policy before receiving treatment whenever possible, so they can arrange direct billing and avoid you having to front thousands of dollars.
