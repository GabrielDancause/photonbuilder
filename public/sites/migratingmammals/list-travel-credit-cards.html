Why Avoid Foreign Transaction Fees (FX Fees)?

A foreign transaction fee is a surcharge—typically 3%—that credit card issuers charge when you make a purchase that passes through a foreign bank or is in a currency other than the U.S. dollar. If you spend $5,000 on a trip abroad, a standard 3% FX fee would cost you an extra $150 just for the privilege of swiping your card.

For digital nomads, frequent travelers, or anyone traveling internationally, holding a card with No Foreign Transaction Fees (0% FX Fee) is non-negotiable. It is the absolute minimum requirement for any travel wallet.

Visa/Mastercard vs. American Express Abroad

While American Express offers some of the most premium travel perks and lounge networks globally, it suffers from a significant drawback for international travelers: acceptance.

  • Visa and Mastercard: Accepted almost everywhere credit cards are taken globally.
  • American Express: Widely accepted in major global hubs, high-end hotels, and tourist districts, but acceptance drops rapidly in smaller towns, local restaurants, and certain countries (especially in parts of Europe and Asia) due to higher merchant processing fees.

If you carry an Amex (like the Platinum or Gold), you must carry a backup Visa or Mastercard with no foreign transaction fees to ensure you are never caught unable to pay.

How to Choose the Best Travel Card

Choosing the right card depends entirely on your travel habits:

  • Occasional Travelers (1-2 trips/year): Look for mid-tier cards ($95 fee) like the Chase Sapphire Preferred or no-fee options like the Bilt Mastercard. You get solid protections and multipliers without paying for perks you won't use.
  • Frequent Flyers: Premium cards ($395+ fee) like the Capital One Venture X or Amex Platinum make sense. The lounge access, travel credits, and elite statuses easily offset the high annual fees if utilized.
  • Cash Back vs. Points: Travel points (Chase UR, Amex MR, Capital One Miles) can yield massive value if transferred to airline partners for business class flights. If you prefer simplicity, a no-fee cash back card like the SavorOne might be better.
