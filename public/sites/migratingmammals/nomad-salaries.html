Frequently Asked Questions

What is the highest paying remote job?
Based on our 2026 data, specialized engineering (like Frontend and DevOps) and senior finance roles are the highest paying remote positions, often exceeding $140,000 USD in median base salary.
How much do remote software engineers make?
Remote software engineers typically earn a median salary between $98,000 and $145,000 depending on their specific domain (Frontend, Backend, Full Stack, DevOps) and experience level.
Are remote jobs paying less in 2026?
While the massive hiring spikes of previous years have stabilized, top-tier remote roles still command premium compensation. However, increased global competition means companies often have more leverage, especially for entry-level positions.
Which non-tech remote jobs pay the most?
Sales Representatives, Customer Success Managers, and Finance Managers are among the highest paying non-engineering remote roles, with medians frequently crossing the $140,000 threshold.
Where does this salary data come from?
Data is algorithmically aggregated from major remote job boards including RemoteOK, We Work Remotely, and Remotive. We specifically filter for listings that provide explicit, transparent salary ranges to calculate our medians and percentiles.

Methodology

We collected data by scraping and parsing live remote job listings across major platforms (RemoteOK, We Work Remotely, and Remotive). We specifically targeted listings that provided explicit salary ranges or base compensation figures.

For each matched role, we calculated the median, 25th percentile, and 75th percentile based on the average of the provided salary ranges. Roles with insufficient data points or without transparent salary listings were excluded from the final results to ensure accuracy over speculation (null over fake data).

All figures are presented in USD and generally represent Mid-to-Senior level base compensation, excluding equity, bonuses, or localized cost-of-living adjustments unless explicitly stated in the listing.
