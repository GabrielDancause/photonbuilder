Why Convert Volume to Weight?

In professional kitchens and bakeries, measuring by weight (grams or ounces) is the standard. Measuring cups are inherently inaccurate for dry ingredients like flour because how you scoop can drastically change the amount packed into the cup. By converting your volume recipes to weight, you ensure perfect consistency every time you bake.

How Does Ingredient Density Work?

Different ingredients have different densities. One cup of water weighs approximately 236 grams, but one cup of all-purpose flour only weighs about 125 grams because it contains air. One cup of honey is extremely dense and weighs about 340 grams.

When you convert between a volume unit (like cups or tablespoons) and a weight unit (like grams or ounces), our calculator asks you to specify the ingredient so it can apply the correct density multiplier.

Common Culinary Conversions

  • 1 cup = 16 tablespoons = 48 teaspoons ≈ 237 ml
  • 1 tablespoon = 3 teaspoons ≈ 15 ml
  • 1 ounce (weight) ≈ 28.35 grams
  • 1 pound = 16 ounces ≈ 454 grams
  • 1 stick of butter = ½ cup = 4 ounces = 113 grams

Temperature: Fahrenheit to Celsius

Most American recipes use Fahrenheit, while the rest of the world uses Celsius. Common oven temperatures:

  • Warm: 200°F = 95°C
  • Low: 300°F = 150°C
  • Moderate: 350°F = 175°C (Standard baking temp)
  • Moderately High: 375°F = 190°C
  • High: 400°F = 205°C
  • Very High: 450°F = 230°C
