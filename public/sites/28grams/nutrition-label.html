What is a Nutrition Label?

The Nutrition Facts label is required by the FDA (Food and Drug Administration) on most packaged foods and beverages in the United States. It provides detailed information about a food's nutrient content, such as the amount of fat, sugar, sodium and fiber it has.

How Daily Values (DV%) are Calculated

The Daily Values are reference amounts of nutrients to consume or not to exceed each day. The %DV shows how much a nutrient in a single serving of a food or dietary supplement contributes to your daily diet.

  • Total Fat: 78g (100% DV)
  • Saturated Fat: 20g (100% DV)
  • Cholesterol: 300mg (100% DV)
  • Sodium: 2,300mg (100% DV)
  • Total Carbohydrate: 275g (100% DV)
  • Dietary Fiber: 28g (100% DV)
  • Added Sugars: 50g (100% DV)
  • Vitamin D: 20mcg (100% DV)
  • Calcium: 1,300mg (100% DV)
  • Iron: 18mg (100% DV)
  • Potassium: 4,700mg (100% DV)
