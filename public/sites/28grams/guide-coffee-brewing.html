Pour-Over (V60 / Chemex)

Grind Size
Medium-Fine
Like table salt
Standard Ratio
1:15 to 1:17
1g coffee : 16g water
Water Temp
90°C - 96°C (195°F - 205°F)
Flavor Profile
Clean, bright, and nuanced. Highlights acidity and complex floral/fruity notes.
  • Place filter in dripper and rinse with hot water. Discard rinse water.
  • Add ground coffee and level the bed.
  • The Bloom: Pour water equal to 2-3x the coffee weight. Wait 30-45 seconds.
  • The Pour: Slowly pour remaining water in slow, concentric circles.
  • Allow the water to drain completely. Target brew time: 2:30 - 3:30.

French Press

Grind Size
Coarse
Like sea salt
Standard Ratio
1:12 to 1:15
1g coffee : 15g water
Water Temp
93°C (200°F)
Flavor Profile
Heavy, full-bodied, and bold. Retains natural oils for a rich mouthfeel.
  • Preheat the press with hot water, then discard.
  • Add coarse coffee grounds to the carafe.
  • Pour water vigorously to saturate all grounds.
  • Wait 4 minutes to steep.
  • Optional: Gently break the crust and skim off foam/floating bits.
  • Press the plunger down slowly and pour immediately to stop extraction.

AeroPress

Grind Size
Medium-Fine
Slightly finer than pour-over
Standard Ratio
1:15
Highly adaptable
Water Temp
80°C - 85°C (175°F - 185°F)
Flavor Profile
Smooth, versatile, and low acidity. Can mimic both espresso and pour-over styles.
  • Assemble AeroPress upside-down (plunger on bottom) and pre-heat.
  • Place filter in cap and rinse.
  • Add coffee and start timer. Pour water to saturate grounds.
  • Stir gently for 10 seconds. Wait until 1:30.
  • Attach filter cap tightly.
  • Carefully flip the entire assembly onto a sturdy mug.
  • Press plunger steadily for 30 seconds until you hear a hiss.

Espresso

Grind Size
Very Fine
Like powdered sugar
Standard Ratio
1:2
18g in, 36g out
Water Temp
93°C (200°F)
Flavor Profile
Intense, syrupy, highly concentrated with a rich crema.
  • Grind coffee directly into portafilter.
  • Use WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique) to break up clumps and distribute grounds evenly.
  • Tamp grounds firmly and perfectly level.
  • Lock portafilter into group head and start extraction immediately.
  • Aim for 25-30 seconds of extraction time to reach your target yield (e.g., 36g).

The Golden Rules of Coffee Brewing

Regardless of the brewing method you choose, a few fundamental principles dictate the quality of your cup:

  • Freshness matters: Buy whole beans roasted within the last 2-4 weeks. Grind them immediately before brewing. Pre-ground coffee loses its aromatic compounds incredibly fast.
  • Use a scale: Volumetric measurements (like scoops) are wildly inaccurate due to bean density variations. Weighing your coffee and water in grams ensures repeatability.
  • Water quality: Coffee is 98% water. If your tap water tastes bad or is too hard/soft, your coffee will suffer. Use filtered water.

Understanding Extraction

Extraction is the process of dissolving coffee flavors into water. The goal is a balanced extraction. If you under-extract, the coffee tastes sour, salty, and thin. If you over-extract, it tastes bitter, dry, and astringent.

You can control extraction by manipulating three main variables:

  • Grind Size: Finer grinds extract faster (more surface area). Coarser grinds extract slower.
  • Time: The longer the water is in contact with the coffee, the more it extracts.
  • Temperature: Hotter water extracts faster and more efficiently than cooler water.

Dialing In Your Brew

If your coffee doesn't taste right, only change one variable at a time. The easiest variable to change is grind size. If your coffee is sour (under-extracted), grind finer. If your coffee is bitter (over-extracted), grind coarser. Leave your ratio and temperature identical until you have found the optimal grind size.
