Why Measure Baking Ingredients by Weight?

In baking, precision is everything. A recipe measured in volume (like cups) can yield wildly different results depending on how the ingredients are packed. For instance, a scooped cup of flour can weigh anywhere from 100 grams to 150 grams depending on whether it was sifted or packed tightly. Weighing ingredients in grams guarantees consistency and accuracy every single time you bake.

How Ingredient Density Affects Conversions

Every ingredient has a unique density. While 1 cup of water weighs exactly 237 grams (and equals 237 ml), 1 cup of all-purpose flour weighs around 120 grams. Conversely, dense ingredients like honey weigh significantly more (around 340 grams per cup). Our calculator automatically applies these specific density multipliers when you convert between volume and weight.

Understanding Baker's Percentages

Baker's percentage is a standard notation used in bread baking where every ingredient is expressed as a percentage of the total flour weight. The total flour is always set to 100%.

  • If a recipe calls for 1000g of flour and 700g of water, the hydration (water percentage) is 70%.
  • If it calls for 20g of salt, the salt percentage is 2%.
  • This system allows bakers to easily scale recipes up or down while maintaining the exact same dough characteristics.
