What is Baker's Percentage?

  
Baker's percentage is a mathematical method widely used in baking to calculate the amounts of ingredients. Instead of expressing recipes in cups or absolute weights, every ingredient is expressed as a percentage of the total flour weight.

  
This means Total Flour is always exactly 100%. If a recipe has multiple flours (e.g., 80% bread flour, 20% whole wheat), their percentages add up to 100%.


  
Why Use It?

  
    
    
  • Scalability: You can easily scale a recipe up or down by simply changing the total flour weight. All other ingredients scale proportionally.
    • 
    
  • Consistency: It helps visualize the structure of the dough. For example, knowing a dough is 75% hydration (water) gives you an immediate idea of how sticky or workable it will be.
    • 
    
  • Troubleshooting: Comparing recipes is much easier when they are normalized into percentages.
    • 
  


  
How to Use This Tool

  
This calculator is fully reactive. You can:

  
    
    
  • Scale a recipe: Change the "Total Flour" or "Total Dough" weight at the top. Everything else will update.
    • 
    
  • Develop a recipe: Enter your ingredients and tweak their percentages. The gram amounts will calculate automatically based on the total flour.
    • 
    
  • Convert a recipe: If you have a recipe in grams, type the gram amounts into the weight columns. The percentages will be calculated for you.
