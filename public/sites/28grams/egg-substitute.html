Choosing the Right Egg Substitute

  
Eggs play multiple roles in baking: they bind ingredients, add moisture, and help goods rise. Choosing the best substitute depends on what the egg is primarily doing in your recipe.

  
    
    
  • Flax or Chia Eggs: Excellent binders with a slightly nutty flavor. Best for heavy, dense baked goods like muffins, quick breads, and cookies. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes to thicken before using.
    • 
    
  • Applesauce & Mashed Banana: Add great moisture and dense texture, but don't bind as well. Perfect for cakes, brownies, and moist breads. Keep in mind they will add a slight fruit flavor (especially banana).
    • 
    
  • Aquafaba (Chickpea Water): The best overall substitute for egg whites. It can even be whipped into stiff peaks for meringues and macarons. 3 tablespoons equals one whole egg, while 2 tablespoons equals one egg white.
    • 
  


  
When Not to Substitute

  
While substitutes work brilliantly in many recipes, they have limitations. As a general rule, if a recipe calls for more than 3 or 4 eggs, it relies too heavily on eggs for structure, and a substitute may result in a dense or crumbly bake. Substitutes also won't work for egg-centric dishes like omelets or quiches.


  
Baking Tips for Vegan Substitutes

  
When using fruit purées like applesauce or banana, you might want to add an extra ½ teaspoon of baking powder to help the recipe rise, as fruit purées tend to make baked goods heavier. Always use unsweetened applesauce, or reduce the sugar in your recipe to compensate.
