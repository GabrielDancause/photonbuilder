What is Net Worth?

Your net worth is the most accurate measure of your overall financial health. It represents the value of everything you own (your assets) minus the value of everything you owe (your liabilities). Unlike your income, which only shows what you earn, your net worth provides a comprehensive picture of your financial standing at any given point in time.

How to Calculate Your Net Worth

Calculating your net worth is straightforward. Just follow these steps:

  • Add up your assets: Include the balances in your checking and savings accounts, the current market value of your investment portfolios, your retirement accounts, the estimated value of any real estate you own, and the value of large personal assets like vehicles.
  • Add up your liabilities: Tally the outstanding balances on your mortgage, auto loans, student loans, credit card debt, and any other personal loans or debts.
  • Subtract liabilities from assets: The resulting number is your net worth. It can be positive (you own more than you owe) or negative (you owe more than you own).

Why Tracking Net Worth Matters

Consistently tracking your net worth is essential for achieving long-term financial goals, such as saving for retirement or reaching financial independence (FIRE).

  • Measures True Progress: A high income does not necessarily equate to wealth if you are spending it all. Net worth shows if you are actually building wealth over time.
  • Highlights Debt: It forces you to confront your liabilities and motivates you to pay down high-interest debt, which directly increases your net worth.
  • Informs Financial Decisions: Knowing your net worth helps you make better decisions about big purchases, investments, and your overall budget.
