Why Choose a No-Fee Credit Card?

A no-annual-fee credit card is often the foundation of a smart personal finance strategy. By avoiding yearly charges, these cards cost you nothing to hold, making them excellent long-term keeper cards. Maintaining a credit card for a long period without closing it improves the average age of your credit accounts, which is a major factor in calculating a high credit score.

While premium credit cards with high annual fees offer extravagant travel perks and lounge access, no-fee cards have become incredibly competitive. Today, you can easily earn 2% cash back on all purchases or up to 5% in specific categories without ever paying a dime in fees.

Cashback vs. Travel Rewards

When selecting a no-fee card, you generally have to choose between a cashback strategy and a travel rewards strategy:

  • Cashback Cards: These provide absolute flexibility. Your rewards are literal cash that can be used to pay your credit card bill, invested (like with the Fidelity Rewards Visa), or transferred to your checking account. They are simple to understand and guarantee a fixed return.
  • Travel Rewards Cards: Cards like the Capital One VentureOne earn miles or points. While they sometimes offer a lower base earning rate, their points can often be transferred to airline and hotel partners for outsized value. Furthermore, no-fee travel cards are much more likely to waive foreign transaction fees.

Maximizing Your Rewards Strategy

To maximize your return without paying annual fees, many financial enthusiasts build a "wallet" of complementary no-fee cards. For instance, you could use the Citi Double Cash to earn 2% on everyday, un-categorized spending, while using the Amex Blue Cash Everyday exclusively for 3% back at supermarkets and gas stations. Just be sure to pay your statement balance in full every month—the interest paid on a carried balance will rapidly erase any rewards you earn.
