Snowball vs. Avalanche Strategy

When you have multiple debts, choosing the right payoff strategy can help you stay motivated and save money. The two most popular methods are the Debt Snowball and the Debt Avalanche.

Both methods require you to make the minimum payment on all your debts. The difference is where you put your extra money.

  • The Avalanche Method: You target the debt with the highest interest rate first. Once that debt is paid off, you take the money you were paying toward it and apply it to the debt with the next highest interest rate. This mathematically saves you the most money on interest and usually results in the fastest payoff.
  • The Snowball Method: You target the debt with the lowest balance first, regardless of the interest rate. Paying off small debts quickly gives you a psychological "win," building momentum and motivation to tackle the larger balances next.

The Power of Extra Payments

Making only the minimum payments on your credit cards and loans is a trap that can keep you in debt for decades. Minimum payments are designed to cover mostly interest, barely touching the principal balance.

By adding an extra monthly payment—even as little as $50 or $100—you directly reduce the principal. As your principal shrinks, less interest accumulates the following month, creating a compounding effect in reverse. Over time, extra payments can shave years off your payoff timeline and save you thousands of dollars in interest.

How to Use This Calculator

Enter all your current debts, including credit cards, student loans, auto loans, and personal loans. Input the total balance, current interest rate, and your minimum monthly payment for each.

Next, enter any extra money you can commit to paying each month above your minimums. Toggle between the Snowball and Avalanche strategies to see which approach works best for your situation. The calculator will show you exactly when you will become debt-free and how much money and time you will save compared to paying just the minimums.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a debt payoff calculator?

A debt payoff calculator is a tool that helps you determine how long it will take to become debt-free by calculating your payoff timeline based on your current balances, interest rates, and monthly payments.

How does a debt snowball calculator work?

A debt snowball calculator organizes your debts from smallest balance to largest. You pay the minimum on all debts while applying any extra money to the smallest balance first, giving you quick wins.

What is the difference between debt avalanche vs snowball?

The debt avalanche method targets the debt with the highest interest rate first to save the most money. The debt snowball method targets the lowest balance first for psychological momentum. Both strategies require paying minimums on all other debts.

Which strategy saves more money?

Mathematically, the debt avalanche strategy will always save you more money on interest and typically results in a faster overall payoff time.

How much can extra payments help?

Even a small extra monthly payment can significantly reduce your principal balance over time, which means less interest accumulates and can shave years off your payoff timeline.
