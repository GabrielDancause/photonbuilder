Methodology

Coast FIRE is calculated by determining your Full FIRE Number (25 times your annual expenses, based on the 4% Rule) and discounting it back to your current age using an expected real rate of return.

We calculate the real return rate mathematically as: ((1 + Nominal Return) / (1 + Inflation)) - 1. This approach is more accurate than simply subtracting inflation from the nominal return.

All values in the chart and table are shown in Today's Dollars. This is why the Full FIRE target line is flat instead of sloping upwards with inflation. It allows you to conceptualize your future purchasing power based on what money is worth to you right now.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Coast FIRE?

Coast FIRE is the milestone where your existing investments are large enough to compound and reach your full FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) number by traditional retirement age, without requiring you to invest another dime.

Can I stop working if I hit Coast FIRE?

No. Coast FIRE means you can stop saving for retirement. You still need to generate enough income to cover your current day-to-day living expenses until you actually retire. Many people use this milestone to downshift to a lower-stress or part-time job.

Why use 25x expenses for the FIRE Number?

The 25x multiplier is derived from the "4% Rule," a rule of thumb based on the Trinity Study. It suggests that if you withdraw 4% of your starting portfolio value annually, adjusted for inflation, your money has a very high probability of lasting 30 years or more.

Why is the FIRE number line flat on the chart?

We calculate everything in "Real" (inflation-adjusted) terms. By viewing your future portfolio in today's purchasing power, your target number stays constant. If we used nominal terms, both your portfolio and your target would curve upwards steeply, making it harder to read the chart.

What if my current investments are below the Coast FIRE number?

If you haven't hit Coast FIRE yet, you simply need to keep contributing to your investments! The gap between your current savings and the Coast FIRE target shows roughly how much more you need to aggressively save before you can "coast."
