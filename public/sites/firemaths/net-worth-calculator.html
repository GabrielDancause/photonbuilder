Understanding Your Net Worth

Your net worth is the ultimate scorecard of your financial health. While income dictates your lifestyle, net worth dictates your freedom. Utilizing a net worth calculator helps you track progress, ensure you aren't over-leveraged in debt, and plan effectively for financial independence.

How to Calculate Net Worth

The formula is simple: Assets - Liabilities = Net Worth.

First, tally up everything of value you own (assets). This includes liquid cash, checking/savings accounts, retirement accounts (401k, IRA), brokerage investments, real estate equity, and the market value of vehicles or valuables.

Next, sum up everything you owe (liabilities). This includes your remaining mortgage balance, student loans, auto loans, credit card debt, and personal loans. Subtract your liabilities from your assets to find your true net worth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is my net worth?

Your net worth is the total value of all your assets (what you own) minus the total of all your liabilities (what you owe). It is the most accurate measure of your true financial health and wealth accumulation over time. If you sold everything you owned and paid off all your debts, the remaining cash would be your net worth.

How to calculate net worth?

To calculate net worth, list all your assets such as cash, investments, real estate, and valuable personal property. Then list all your liabilities like mortgages, student loans, credit card debt, and auto loans. Subtract the total liabilities from the total assets. Our interactive calculator above does this automatically.

Why use a net worth calculator?

A net worth calculator simplifies tracking your financial progress over time, ensuring you don't overlook common assets or liabilities. It provides a quick snapshot of your financial health, helping you make informed decisions about paying down debt versus investing.

What is a good net worth by age?

A common rule of thumb for a suggested net worth is: (Age x Pre-tax Income) / 10. However, medians vary widely by age group. In the US, the median net worth for those under 35 is roughly $39,000, growing to $135,000 for ages 35-44, $247,000 for 45-54, and peaking around $410,000 for ages 65-74.

Should I include my house in my net worth calculator?

Yes, you should include the current market value of your house as an asset, and the remaining mortgage balance as a liability. The difference between the two is your home equity, which directly contributes to your net worth. Some people track "Liquid Net Worth" which excludes the primary residence, but standard net worth includes it.
