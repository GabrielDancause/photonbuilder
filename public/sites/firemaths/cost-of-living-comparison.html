Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cost of living comparison calculator?
A cost of living comparison calculator is a tool that helps you determine how much money you would need to earn in a different city to maintain your current standard of living. It compares the costs of housing, groceries, transportation, and other basic expenses between your current location and a target destination.
How is the cost of living by city calculated?
The cost of living by city is calculated by compiling data on thousands of prices for various goods and services, including rent or mortgage payments, groceries, restaurants, local transportation, and utilities. These are weighted into an index, usually with a major city like New York serving as the baseline (100).
Why is doing a salary comparison between cities important?
A salary comparison between cities is crucial before accepting a job offer or moving, because a higher salary in a new city might actually result in a lower standard of living if the local costs (especially housing and taxes) are significantly higher than where you currently live.
Are housing costs the biggest factor in cost of living differences?
Yes, housing (rent or home prices) is typically the largest single expense for most households and the factor that varies the most drastically between cities. For example, rent in tech hubs or financial centers can be 300% higher than in smaller midwestern cities, while food and utilities might only differ by 10-20%.
How accurate is this cost of living data?
Our calculator uses crowd-sourced and scraped data from major global cost of living databases. While it provides a highly accurate aggregate baseline for comparison, your personal expenses may vary depending on your specific neighborhood choices, consumption habits, and family size.
