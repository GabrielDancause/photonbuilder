Is it cheaper to lease or buy?

The eternal question in personal finance: is it cheaper to lease or buy a car? The answer depends heavily on your timeframe. In the short term (2-3 years), a car lease calculator will often show leasing as the cheaper option due to lower monthly payments. However, over 5 to 7 years, buying almost universally wins out because you eventually stop making payments and own an asset with residual value.

When leasing makes sense vs buying

Despite the long-term math usually favoring purchasing, leasing can make sense in specific scenarios:

  • Business Owners: If you use the car for business, lease payments can often be deducted as a business expense.
  • The "New Car" Preference: If you are absolutely committed to driving a brand new car every 3 years for safety, reliability, or status, leasing is generally a more efficient way to do so than constantly trading in purchased vehicles.
  • Cash Flow Investing: If you take the monthly savings from a lease payment (compared to a higher loan payment) and invest it into assets yielding high returns, you might outpace the financial penalty of leasing. (Though few people actually do this disciplined investing).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it cheaper to lease or buy a car?
Over a short period (3 years), leasing often looks cheaper due to lower monthly payments. However, over 5 to 7 years, buying is almost always cheaper because you eventually own an asset with residual value and eliminate monthly payments.
What is a car lease calculator?
A car lease calculator estimates your monthly lease payment and total out-of-pocket costs by factoring in the vehicle price, money factor (interest rate), lease term, residual value, down payment, and taxes.
How does a lease vs buy car calculator work?
It compares the total cost of ownership for both options. For buying, it calculates loan payments, depreciation, and equity. For leasing, it calculates total lease payments and fees. It then compares the net cost after 3, 5, or 7 years.
Should I lease a car to get a lower monthly payment?
While leasing provides a lower monthly payment, you are continuously paying for the steepest part of a car's depreciation without building any equity. It makes sense for cash flow, but not for long-term wealth building.
When does leasing a car make financial sense?
Leasing makes sense if you can deduct the lease as a business expense, if you strictly want a new car every 3 years and are willing to pay a premium for it, or if you invest the monthly payment savings into high-yield assets.
