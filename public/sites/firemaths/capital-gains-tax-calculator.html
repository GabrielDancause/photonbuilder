How much tax on stock sale?

Figuring out exactly how much tax you owe on a stock sale can be confusing. It fundamentally depends on your holding period (how long you owned the asset), your filing status, and your overall taxable income. In 2026, selling a stock you've held for a year or less triggers short-term capital gains tax, which is identical to your ordinary income tax bracket (up to 37%). Conversely, holding for over a year unlocks long-term capital gains rates, which are significantly lower (0%, 15%, or 20%).

Our capital gains tax calculator factors in these variables to give you a precise breakdown. Remember that federal taxes are only part of the equation—you may also be subject to the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) and state-level capital gains taxes.

Capital gains tax rate 2026

The IRS adjusts tax brackets annually for inflation. For 2026, the long-term capital gains tax rates remain at 0%, 15%, and 20%, but the income thresholds have shifted. If you're a single filer, you can enjoy a 0% federal rate on long-term gains if your total taxable income is under $49,200. The 15% rate applies from $49,201 to $553,850, and anything above that is taxed at 20%.

FAQ

How much tax on stock sale do I pay in 2026?

The tax you pay on a stock sale in 2026 depends on how long you held the asset. If held for one year or less (short-term), it's taxed at your ordinary income tax rate. If held for more than one year (long-term), it's taxed at more favorable rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on your taxable income, plus a potential 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) and state taxes.

What is the capital gains tax rate 2026?

For 2026, the long-term capital gains tax rates are 0%, 15%, or 20%. For a single filer, the 0% rate applies to income up to $49,200; the 15% rate applies from $49,201 to $553,850; and the 20% rate applies above $553,850. Short-term rates align with ordinary income brackets, ranging from 10% to 37%.

How does a capital gains tax calculator work?

A capital gains tax calculator computes your net profit from an asset sale by subtracting the purchase price from the sale price to find the total gain. It then applies the appropriate federal tax bracket based on your income, holding period, and filing status, factors in the 3.8% NIIT if applicable, and adds state-specific capital gains taxes.

Does my state charge capital gains tax on stock sales?

Most US states tax capital gains as ordinary income, meaning state rates can range from 0% (in states like Texas, Florida, and Nevada) to over 13.3% in California. Our calculator automatically applies the correct estimated state tax rate for all 50 states.

What is the 3.8% NIIT surcharge on stock sales?

The Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) is a 3.8% surtax that applies to certain net investment income of individuals, estates, and trusts that have income above the statutory threshold amounts ($200,000 for single filers and $250,000 for married filing jointly).
