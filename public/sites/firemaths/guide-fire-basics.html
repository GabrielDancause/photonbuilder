Types of FIRE

Lean FIRE

Retiring with a minimalist lifestyle, covering only essential expenses. Requires a smaller portfolio but offers less financial cushion or room for luxury.

Target: $40k/yr or less

Fat FIRE

Retiring with a higher-than-average standard of living. Allows for significant discretionary spending (travel, dining, etc.). Requires a much larger portfolio.

Target: $100k/yr or more

Barista FIRE

Quitting the corporate rat race but continuing to work part-time (often for health insurance and basic income) while investments grow or cover the gap.

Target: Partial FI

Steps to Financial Independence

Determine your annual expenses and multiply by 25 (based on the 4% Safe Withdrawal Rate). This is your target portfolio size.

FIRE Number = Annual Expenses × 25

Before investing heavily, pay off credit cards and personal loans. The guaranteed "return" of avoiding 20% interest is unbeatable in the stock market.

Save 3 to 6 months of living expenses in a High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA). This prevents you from needing to sell investments during a market downturn or personal crisis.

Increase income (side hustles, promotions) and decrease expenses (housing, transport, food). The gap between your income and expenses is your wealth-building engine.

Savings Rate = (Savings / Income) × 100

Invest the gap into low-cost, broadly diversified index funds (like VTSAX or VOO). Utilize tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) first, then taxable brokerage accounts.

What is the FIRE Movement?

The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement is a lifestyle and financial strategy aimed at achieving financial freedom long before the traditional retirement age of 65. The core philosophy centers around aggressive savings, intentional spending, and consistent investing.

By saving a high percentage of their income—often 50% or more—followers of the FIRE movement build a portfolio of investments designed to generate enough passive income to cover their living expenses indefinitely.

The Math Behind FIRE

The movement relies heavily on the 4% Rule, derived from the Trinity Study. This rule suggests that a retiree can safely withdraw 4% of their investment portfolio in the first year of retirement, adjusting for inflation in subsequent years, without running out of money over a 30-year period.

To find your target portfolio size, you simply invert the 4% rule by multiplying your annual expenses by 25. For example, if you need $40,000 a year to live, your FIRE number is $1,000,000 ($40,000 × 25).

Is FIRE Right for You?

While the "Retire Early" aspect gets the most attention, many proponents focus primarily on "Financial Independence." Reaching FI means work becomes optional. You gain the freedom to change careers, start a business, or work part-time without worrying about paying the bills.

However, the journey requires discipline. It involves scrutinizing your spending, avoiding lifestyle creep, and weathering stock market volatility. Whether you pursue Lean FIRE, Fat FIRE, or something in between, the principles of living below your means and investing for the future are universally beneficial.
