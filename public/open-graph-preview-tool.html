Frequently Asked Questions

What are Open Graph tags?
Open Graph (OG) tags are specific metadata snippets added to the <head> of a webpage to dictate how the page's content is displayed when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, and Discord. They control elements such as the title, description, and image shown in the link preview.
What is the recommended size for an Open Graph image?
The optimal resolution for an Open Graph image (og:image) is 1200 x 630 pixels. This provides a 1.91:1 aspect ratio, which looks great across most social platforms and ensures your image appears crisp and isn't cropped awkwardly.
Why is my Twitter Card not showing the large image?
To ensure Twitter displays a large image preview, you must include the meta tag <meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">. If you use content="summary", Twitter will only show a small thumbnail. Additionally, verify your image meets Twitter's size requirements.
How long should my Open Graph title and description be?
For og:title, keep it under 60 characters to prevent truncation on most devices. For og:description, aim for 65-90 characters. While some platforms may display more, keeping it concise ensures the core message is visible everywhere.
Do Open Graph tags affect SEO?
While Open Graph tags don't directly impact search engine rankings (like Google), they significantly impact Click-Through Rates (CTR) from social media. A compelling, well-formatted preview drives more traffic to your site, which is a positive signal for overall site engagement.
