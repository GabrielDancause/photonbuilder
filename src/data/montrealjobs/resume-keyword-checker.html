The Black Hole of Job Applications: Understanding ATS for Designers

As a visual designer, you probably spend countless hours perfecting your portfolio, curating your case studies, and ensuring your typography is immaculate. But what happens if the hiring manager never even sees your portfolio?

Enter the Applicant Tracking System (ATS). Over 98% of Fortune 500 companies and a vast majority of mid-sized Montreal agencies and tech companies use an ATS to filter through the hundreds of applications they receive for a single design role.

An ATS doesn't care about your negative space or your color theory. It cares about structured text and keywords. If your resume is a beautifully designed PDF that the ATS cannot parse, or if it lacks the specific keywords the employer has programmed the system to look for, your application is automatically rejected. You are sent into the "black hole" of job hunting.

The Montreal Context: A Unique Design Market

The Montreal job market has its own set of rules, heavily influenced by language laws (like the OQLF's Bill 96), a booming AI sector, and a deep-rooted creative culture.

The Bilingual Resume Dilemma

Should you send a French resume, an English resume, or a bilingual one? In Montreal, the answer is often context-dependent, but from an ATS perspective, clarity is key.

  • For Francophone Agencies (e.g., LG2, Cossette): Always submit a pristine French resume. The ATS will be looking for terms like "Designer Graphique", "Mise en page", and "Identité visuelle".
  • For Tech Startups / International Companies (e.g., Coveo, Lightspeed): These companies often use global ATS platforms (like Greenhouse or Lever) configured in English. An English resume is usually preferred unless stated otherwise.
  • The Hybrid Approach: Do not split your resume straight down the middle with French on the left and English on the right. An ATS reads left-to-right, top-to-bottom. It will read "Education Éducation Bachelor of Arts Baccalauréat en Arts" and turn your experience into gibberish. Instead, create two separate resumes and submit the one that matches the language of the job posting.

Critical Montreal Keywords

Regardless of the language, Montreal employers are actively looking for language proficiencies. Ensure these keywords are explicitly stated on your resume:

English: Bilingual, Fluent in French and English, Written and Spoken French

French: Bilingue, Maîtrise du français et de l'anglais, Bilinguisme (français/anglais)

Top Keywords for Graphic Designers in 2026

Keyword optimization isn't about stuffing your resume with invisible white text (a myth that will actually get you blacklisted by modern ATS). It's about naturally incorporating the skills employers demand into your work experience bullet points.

1. The AI Revolution (The Highest Premium)

As noted on our Market Analysis page, AI skills carry a 15-25% salary premium. Traditional graphic design is evolving, and employers want to see that you are adapting. Key terms to include:

  • Generative AI, Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion
  • Prompt Engineering, AI Workflows, Automation
  • Adobe Firefly, Photoshop Generative Fill
  • AI-assisted Video (Runway, Pika)

2. Industry Standard Software & Tools

Don't just write "Adobe Creative Suite." List the specific programs, as the ATS is often looking for exact matches:

  • Figma (Crucial for modern collaborative workflows, even for print designers)
  • Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign
  • After Effects, Premiere Pro (Motion graphics are increasingly expected)
  • Webflow, WordPress, Shopify (For digital designers)

3. Methodologies and Concepts

These terms show you understand how design fits into the broader business context:

  • UI/UX, User Experience, User Interface
  • Responsive Design, Mobile-First
  • Accessibility, WCAG, AODA
  • Agile, Scrum, Cross-functional collaboration
  • Brand Identity, Typography, Color Theory, Layout
  • A/B Testing, Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

How to Format Your Resume for ATS Scanners

If you take one thing away from this guide, let it be this: Keep your ATS resume aggressively simple. You can link to your beautifully designed portfolio, and you can bring a highly designed resume to the actual interview. But the file you upload to Workday or Taleo must follow these rules:

  1. No Columns: ATS parsers read straight across. Columns will scramble your text. Use a single-column layout.
  2. Standard Headings: Use traditional names for your sections. Use "Work Experience" or "Expérience Professionnelle" instead of "My Journey" or "Where I've Been." Use "Education" instead of "Academic Background."
  3. No Images, Charts, or Icons: That infographic showing your Photoshop skills at 90%? The ATS cannot read it. It will register your Photoshop skill as non-existent. Use text.
  4. Standard Fonts: Stick to web-safe fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Helvetica. Avoid custom downloaded fonts for the ATS version.
  5. File Format: Unless the application specifically asks for a PDF, a standard Microsoft Word document (.docx) is the most reliably parsed file format for older ATS systems. Modern systems (Greenhouse, Lever) handle PDFs perfectly, but Word is the safest bet universally.
