Methodology

This calculator simulates the long-term impact of a Dividend Reinvestment Plan (DRIP). It projects the future value of an investment by accounting for both share price appreciation and the compounding effect of reinvested dividends.

Assumptions:

  • Dividends are paid and reinvested annually at the end of each year.
  • Taxes on dividends are not factored in. In taxable accounts, dividends are usually taxed even if reinvested, which would reduce the actual reinvestable amount.
  • Fractional shares are permitted for reinvestment.
  • Price appreciation and dividend growth occur at a constant rate specified by the user.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a DRIP?

A Dividend Reinvestment Plan (DRIP) allows investors to automatically reinvest cash dividends received from a stock into additional shares or fractional shares of the underlying company, rather than taking the dividend in cash.

Why is DRIP powerful?

DRIP harnesses the power of compound interest. When you reinvest dividends, you buy more shares. Those new shares then generate their own dividends in the future, accelerating the growth of your share count and total income over time.

Should I use a DRIP?

If you are in the accumulation phase of your investing journey and do not need the current income from your investments, using a DRIP is generally an excellent way to automatically build wealth over time without incurring transaction fees at most modern brokerages.
