Frequently Asked Questions

What is compound interest?

Compound interest is the interest on savings calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. In simple terms, it's "interest on interest." This creates a snowball effect where your wealth grows at an accelerating rate over time.

How does compounding frequency affect my returns?

The more frequently interest is compounded, the more money you make. For example, monthly compounding will yield slightly higher returns than annual compounding over the same period, because the interest is added to your principal more often, allowing that new interest to start earning interest sooner.

Methodology

This calculator assumes that contributions are made at the end of each period (e.g., at the end of each month for monthly contributions). The interest is calculated based on the selected compounding frequency. The "Total Interest" column shows the cumulative interest earned since year zero.
