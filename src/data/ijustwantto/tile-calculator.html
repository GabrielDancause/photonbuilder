How to Calculate Floor and Wall Tiles

Estimating the number of tiles you need for a project requires more than just knowing the square footage of your space. To get an accurate count, you need to calculate the area of a single tile, factoring in the grout joint, and then divide the total project area by this single tile area. The formula is: Total Tiles = Project Area / (((Tile Length + Grout Size) × (Tile Width + Grout Size)) / 144).

Always ensure your measurements are in the same unit before doing the math. Our calculator converts the tile dimensions from inches to square feet to perfectly match your project's square footage.

Why is a Waste Factor Important?

No room is perfectly square, and mistakes happen. Adding a waste factor ensures you have enough tiles to complete your project without waiting weeks for a new batch to arrive (which might not even perfectly match the original dye lot).

  • 10% Waste Factor: The standard recommendation for most rooms with simple layouts and straight walls.
  • 15% Waste Factor: Recommended for rooms with complex angles, numerous obstacles (like a bathroom with a tub, toilet, and vanity), or if you are installing the tiles in a diagonal or herringbone pattern.
  • 20% Waste Factor: Often necessary for very complex layouts or when using fragile natural stone tiles that may break more easily during cutting.

How Grout Size Affects Your Tile Estimate

Grout lines may seem small, but over a large floor, they can significantly reduce the number of tiles you actually need. For instance, using a 1/2" grout joint instead of a 1/16" joint on a large patio will mean buying fewer tiles overall. By including the grout joint in your calculation, you avoid overestimating the number of tiles required, saving you money on your project.
