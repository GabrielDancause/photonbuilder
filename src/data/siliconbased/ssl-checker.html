What is an SSL Certificate?

An SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate is a digital certificate that authenticates a website's identity and enables an encrypted connection. It ensures that all data passed between the web server and browsers remain private and integral. Certificates contain the website's public key and identity information, and are issued by a trusted Certificate Authority (CA).

How to read PEM Certificates

PEM (Privacy-Enhanced Mail) is the most common format for X.509 certificates. They are Base64 encoded ASCII files and have specific header and footer lines, typically starting with -----BEGIN CERTIFICATE----- and ending with -----END CERTIFICATE-----. A single PEM file can contain one certificate or an entire certificate chain (server certificate, intermediate CA certificates, and optionally the root CA certificate).

Certificate Grades & Expiry

This tool evaluates your certificate validity based on its dates:

  • Grade A: Certificate is valid and has more than 30 days until expiration.
  • Grade B: Certificate is valid but expires in less than 30 days.
  • Grade C: Certificate is valid but expires very soon (less than 7 days).
  • Grade F: Certificate is expired or not yet valid.

Client-Side Privacy

Our SSL Certificate Checker runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript. When you paste your certificate (which typically only contains public information), it is parsed locally. No data is sent to any server. This ensures maximum privacy, speed, and full offline capability.
